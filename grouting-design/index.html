Pasadena sits on the eastern edge of the Los Angeles Basin, where the Raymond Fault and several minor strands cut through Quaternary alluvium and older fan deposits. The groundwater table fluctuates between 15 and 40 feet, and the sandy gravels with silt lenses make loose zones common. Grouting design in Pasadena California must address these variable conditions, especially where excavations intersect the fault zone or where old stream channels create preferential flow paths. Before any injection program, we recommend a permeability field test to map hydraulic conductivity and a georadar survey to identify subsurface voids. These preliminary steps ensure the grout mix and pressure match the actual ground behavior, not a generic assumption.

Grouting design in Pasadena California must account for variable alluvial soils and fault-related fractures that can redirect grout flow unpredictably.

Critical ground factors in Pasadena California

The Raymond Fault has produced several moderate earthquakes in historical time, and the alluvial basin amplifies ground motion. For a grouting design in Pasadena California, the main risk is uncontrolled grout travel through open fractures or coarse gravel beds. If the injection pressure exceeds the overburden stress, the ground can hydraulically fracture, wasting material and potentially damaging adjacent structures. Another risk is that grout may bypass the target zone entirely if the permeability contrast between layers is too high. We mitigate this by performing a pre-grout infiltration test to define the flow regime and by adjusting the rheology of the mix based on the measured soil gradation.

Our services

Our grouting design services in Pasadena California cover three main approaches, each suited to different ground conditions and project goals.

Permeation Grouting Design

For sandy gravels and silty sands, we design low-pressure permeation grouting with particle size analysis to ensure the grout penetrates without clogging the pore spaces. The mix design considers the d10 and d15 of the soil, and the injection pattern follows a triangular grid with hole spacing of 4 to 6 feet.

Compaction Grouting Design

When loose fill or collapsible soils are present, we use stiff, low-slump grout injected as a bulb to densify the ground. This method works well in Pasadena California where old stream channels left loose zones beneath existing structures. The design includes volume control and heave monitoring to protect adjacent foundations.

Chemical Grouting Design

For fine sands and silts where cement grout cannot penetrate, we design chemical grout programs using sodium silicate or acrylate with controlled gel times. Each batch is tested in the field for viscosity and gel time before injection, and the program is adjusted as the injection progresses.

Common questions

What is the typical cost range for a grouting design project in Pasadena California?

For a residential or small commercial site, the design and field injection program typically ranges from US$1.130 to US$4.660, depending on the volume of grout, number of injection points, and depth of treatment. Larger sites with multiple stages can exceed this range.

How long does a grouting design study take in Pasadena California?

A standard program takes 3 to 7 days in the field, plus 2 to 3 weeks for laboratory testing of the grout mix and final report preparation. The timeline depends on site access, weather, and whether preliminary permeability tests are needed.

What soil conditions in Pasadena California require grouting design?

The alluvial soils in the Raymond Basin include loose sandy gravels, silt lenses, and occasional clay layers that can settle under load. Grouting is also used where excavations encounter the fault zone, where fractured bedrock below the alluvium needs sealing, or where old underground utilities left voids.

Do you follow specific ASTM standards for grouting design?

Yes. We design grouting programs in accordance with ASTM D5092 for grout seals, ASTM C109 for compressive strength of cementitious grouts, and the ASCE Grouting Committee guidelines for permeation and compaction grouting. All field tests use calibrated equipment with current certificates.

Can grouting be performed under an existing building in Pasadena California?

Yes. Compaction grouting and chemical grouting can be done from the ground surface or through the slab with minimal disturbance. We design the injection sequence and monitor heave with tilt sensors and survey targets to ensure no structural damage occurs during the operation.
